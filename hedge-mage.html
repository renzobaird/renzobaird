HEDGE MAGE

ROLE

Game System Designer, Level Designer, Narrative Designer, Programmer, Co-Artist, UI/UX Designer

ABOUT

Hedge Mage is a single player horror-fantasy hedge maze pixel-art game where the player navigates a dark maze to locate letters to complete the spell to escape.

Tasked with the objective to make an arcade-style game designed around typography, we set off to create a game inspired by Pacman and classic horror games. Our goals were to create an immersive experience with a unique art style, engaging gameplay mechanics, and a captivating narrative.

UI/UX Design

1/4

Typography

As a co-typographer, we set out to design a typeface that paid homage to fantasy and horror. With the core mechanic of the game centered around the collection of letters, it was very important to us to get it right.

The long vertical stress and angular geometry provide our horror elements, whereas our ornate terminals give it a fantastical finish that brings in fantasy. The typeface feels as though it belongs perfectly to a pixel-art spell book for our wizard character.

2/4

HUD

I am currently prototyping interactive HUD elements and menu systems for Hedge Mage with Alaren Studios. As the lead UI/UX Designer, I am working with our programmer and artist to develop a system that feels meaningful and unintrusive.

With that said, you can see a prototype of ways we are looking to incorporate player lives and health that feel additive to the fantasy-horror components of the game.

3/4

Menus & Book

I knew early in the game’s development that a spell book would be part of our core mechanics. The player will track their level progression, game progression, spell information, spell casting, game menus, and even creature information.

To stay on par with the horror aspect of the game, the usage of the book does not stop gameplay. The intention for this is to force the player to use the book strategically. This meant that we had to have easy ways to navigate the book and simple layouts.

Game Design

1/4

Game Mechanics

This was my first game I designed from the very beginning. I wanted to emulate Pacman's gameplay style and mechanics, but with a horror twist. I designed the core mechanics around collecting letters to form a spell to escape the maze while avoiding enemies.

2/4

Level Design

The level designed changed many times over the course of development to improve flow and challenge. In the design to the left, we focused on creating a central plaza, more nooks and crannies for exploration, and strategic letter placements to improve the player experience.

Playtesting was crucial in refining the level design to ensure it was engaging and fun. Current development is focused on the addition of challenging navigation, narrative elements,how the player's spells (abilities) interact with the level, and overall pacing.

3/4

System Balancing

The original version of the game focused on simple core mechanics that were intuitive and easy to understand. As development progressed, it became clear that the game lacked emergent gameplay. As the game got picked up by AET's Capstone Course - Indie Game Studio, the team shifted focus in creating creature diversification, player abilities, and UI enhancements.

4/4

Gameplay Flow

Currently the game is in development with a sole focus on improved gameplay and flow. I am evaluating every system in relation to the overall player experience and pacing. I am desiging an all new UI Book interaction that adds new features for the player. New creatures are in development that focus on more relationships to the player than just health and lives. Lastly, I am developing player abilities which will be a Hedge Mage first. Adding in "Spells" that the player can use adds to the narrative/ lore of the game, and gives the player more controls in how they interact with the dark maze, creatures, and the letter system.
